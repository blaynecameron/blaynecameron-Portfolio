    Overview

    Paycrave is a project I did as part of my mentorship with Bloc. It includes wireframes and a clickable prototype for an Android app designed to help users to find and order from food trucks nearby.

    Problem

    Although it was an assignment, it's still a real problem. There are several apps that allow you to find and order from your favourite restaurants. But, you cannot do this with food trucks. There are apps that allow you to find food trucks, but you cannot order food from them.

    User Stories

    The project had several requirements which I had to include in the design. So, I started off organizing the app requirements into a list of user stories.

    User Flows

    Using the user stories, I sketched user flows to get a better idea of how users would navigate through the app and to also help me to start to think the features in greater detail. This included flows for new users and returning users, searching for food trucks and making purchases.

    Branding

    The logo, colour pallette and typography were all included with the project. So it was up to me to implement them in the wireframes.

    Pen and Paper

    The wireframe process for me almost always starts with good old fashioned pen and paper. Through these sketches I was able to determine the general layout of the various screens of the app and also to explore how navigation would feel and look like if a tab bar was used.

    Splash Screens

    Since Paycrave had a Sign In/Up feature I believed it would be a good opportunity to include some splash screens to further educate users on the benefits of the app and also to introduce the brand.

    Focus

    The primary function of the app is to help users find and order from food trucks. But, there were also other features which were important, but did not necessarily deserve the spotlight. This included showing book marked food trucks, purchase history and settings. I decided that it would be better to place these features in a hamburger menu to hide them while keeping the display of food trucks in the forefront. This places the emphasis on the core function of the app making it easier for users to be able to find food trucks and make purchases more easily.

    Reducing Checkout Steps and errors

    Making purchases on smartphones can quickly become a huge pain point for users if there are too many steps involved. Therefore, having fewer steps from the point users select items and pay for them was of the utmost importance.The designs are made to break this down into a two step process from selecting items and making payments.
